Golden Temple

The Golden Temple was built by Guru Arjan Dev Ji in the 16th century. Its architecture is unique because it combines Hindu and Islamic styles. The upper part of the temple is covered with gold, while the lower part is made of white marble. The temple is surrounded by a sacred water tank called Amrit Sarovar, which adds to its beauty and spiritual importance.

The Golden Temple is open to people of all religions, castes, and backgrounds. This reflects the Sikh belief in equality and brotherhood. The four entrances of the temple from all directions symbolize openness and acceptance of everyone.
One of the most special features of the Golden Temple is the Langar, or community kitchen. Here, free meals are served daily to thousands of visitors without any discrimination. The food is prepared and served by volunteers, showing the values of selfless service and humility.
